== MULTI-FILE TASKS (routing rules) ==
- When MULTIPLE files are attached: ChatGPT must do the file analysis first.
  It has the biggest intake (up to 20 files per message on paid; the FREE plan
  allows only a handful of uploads per day, so keep attachments few and vital).
- When multiple files feed a DELIVERABLE (deck, visual layout, design piece):
  prefer Claude Design as the make-stage.
- Claude Design CANNOT edit or process video files - it never receives video.
  Reels from real phone footage are a video-tool job (Runway at best, and even
  Runway restyles single clips rather than editing many clips together).

== VIDEO/REEL TASKS — pick by what the source material actually is ==
- Real footage assembly (business already has phone-shot clips of products,
  clients, sites and wants them cut together with branded intro/outro,
  captions, music, animated overlays, tailored per platform/audience —
  Instagram vs LinkedIn vs an investor pitch): use InVideo AI. It is
  prompt-driven like Prism's other tools and edits the user's OWN uploaded
  clips together rather than generating new ones.
- Pure generated cinematic video (no real footage exists yet, everything is
  AI-imagined motion/scenes): use Runway.
- Deck/design work from documents, images or brand assets (no video
  involved): use Claude Design.
- Don't default every "make a reel/video" request to Runway — ask (or infer
  from attachments) whether real footage exists first. If it does, InVideo AI
  is almost always the better fit.

== NOTEBOOKLM — pick this over a plain research/brains tool when the task is ==
- Explaining/summarizing a LARGE amount of source material at once (many
  files, long documents, a whole project/codebase's worth of context, long
  video transcripts) rather than a single narrow question.
- The deliverable itself is an explainer VIDEO or narrated walkthrough:
  NotebookLM has a built-in "Video Overview" generator (and "Audio Overview",
  podcast-style) built specifically to turn a pile of uploaded source
  material into a spoken/visual explanation — this is a real, distinct
  capability, not just document Q&A.
- Concretely: a request like "explain our whole project/workflow/every tool
  we use, make it a video I can show someone" is a NotebookLM job first, even
  though it looks like a generic "media"/video task.
- NotebookLM is listed under BOTH "research" and "media" for exactly this
  reason — its Video/Audio Overview output IS the finished video, not a
  research brief that still needs a separate video-maker afterwards. When
  the deliverable is an explainer video built FROM a large pile of the
  user's own material, assign NotebookLM as the MEDIA-stage tool directly
  (not Runway/InVideo AI/Claude Design) — those only animate a script or
  restyle real footage, they don't digest and explain source material.
- Still prefer Perplexity/Consensus/WolframAlpha for narrow, single-fact or
  live-web lookups — NotebookLM needs the material uploaded/attached first,
  it doesn't search the open web.
